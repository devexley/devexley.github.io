Affirmation and StereoType Threat

Values

August 2, 2015

Compassion is something I value. I try my best to practice it as it is tantamount to trying to understand someone else's position and emotionally relate to it. It is a keystone I think of having a sense of humanity. It is important to me because it helps you relate to people and their circumstances and provides perspective.

I think this is one of the reasons people often tell me things or share there feelings with me because I am a compassionate listener, or at least try to be. As a result, I'm rarely asked for specific advice, but provide advice through listening and understanding.

In thinking about the values I appreciate, compassion, truth, friendships, I realize that I cherish a sort of mutually supporting environment. I think knowing that my cohort will be in the same situation is ameliorative and, if we can avoid stereotype threat and animosity, the experience will be rich and rewarding.

This will take great effort on my part to stay bouyant and not let negativity sabotage the experience as I am generally a pessimist and usually have that imposter feeling. I'll try to remember it is a chance to learn as much as possible.
